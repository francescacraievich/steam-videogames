Introduction
The Steam gaming market

Are the most watched games on Twitch also the most played on Steam?
  • Correlation between Twitch viewership hours and estimated Steam owners
  • Top 25 titles present on both platforms — real data from Twitch_game_data.csv + Steam dataset
  • Twitch hours aggregated across all months (2016–2023) · color by Category
Twitch viewership vs Steam owners — 25 titles · x-axis capped at 2,000M Twitch Game Data + FronkonGames Steam Dataset (Kaggle)
💡
Take-home message
  • Dota 2 (~4,504M h) and Apex Legends (~2,655M h) are extreme outliers in Twitch viewership — removed from the x-axis for readability.
  • The most-watched titles are predominantly multiplayer or F2P games — they cluster in the high-viewership right side of the chart, often with large Steam ownership too.
  • Single-player games (purple) dominate the left side: most have modest Twitch hours and relatively low Steam ownership — suggesting they are played more than spectated.
  • While a loose positive trend exists, the correlation is weak and non-linear — the wide vertical scatter at any given viewership level suggests that high Twitch viewership does not necessarily translate into more Steam downloads.

Which genre retains players the most?
  • Metric: median playtime forever — median total hours per user, per genre
  • Median preferred over mean: outliers like DOTA 2 and Forza strongly skew the mean
  • 12 main game genres · hover for sample size per genre
Median hours played by genre — all Steam titles Source: FronkonGames Steam Dataset (Kaggle) · CC-BY 4.0
💡
Take-home message
  • Massively Multiplayer retains players the most (6.2h median) — designed for the long run
  • RPG and Strategy follow: deep progression = longer sessions
  • Racing and Free to Play have a very high mean (74h, 81h) but low median — a few viral titles (Forza, DOTA 2) pull the average up
  • Action and Casual have the lowest median: fast consumption, short sessions

Discarded visualization — Q1
Scatter plot: individual price vs rating
▪▪▪▪▪▪▪▪
122,000+ overlapping points → total overplotting
No readable pattern
Why it was discarded
  • Extreme overplotting: 122k points fully overlap, making any pattern unreadable
  • Price is a continuous variable with strong concentrations (€0, €5, €10, €15...) — not uniformly distributed
  • Hard to compare price ranges without aggregation
  • Even with transparency or jitter, density made the chart unclear
Chosen alternative
  • Aggregation into ordered price ranges → overplotting eliminated
  • Bar chart with median → immediate main message (which range is "best")
  • IQR shown in tooltip → detail for those who want to explore further
